Problem Statment

The area of focus in this project work is to identify if a student/working professionals is stressed. Stress is like a disease which affects a human being and due to lack of awareness is mostly ignored. It has serious outcomes and at times can also be fatal leading to an increase in the number of suicide cases. In this paper we present a method to determine whether a person is stressed by continuously determining the emotion of the person for a particular duration of time.





Methodology
Adopted

The Fer2013 dataset is one of the most challenging datasets in the Deep Learning field till date. Hence, to achieve a higher accuracy wrt emotion detection from this dataset would require more than just a simple CNN model.
Data augmentation techniques were used before we trained our model on this dataset due to the under representation of some emotions in the dataset. Training and validation was split into the classic 80:20 ratio.
Xception model was separated into tiny, mini and big exception with varying aspects along with the simple CNN model as well. Each model was trained on the dataset and the one which gave the best accuracy was used.
Once we load the model into the application, we detect the face and extract the required features for stress calculator which is mainly the eyebrows and lips.
Studies have shown that the contraction of lips (lip twitching) is when a person is nervous/stressed and the distance between left and right eyebrow if maximum implies relief and if the eyebrows are contracted, the distance between them is less and hence the person is in stress.
